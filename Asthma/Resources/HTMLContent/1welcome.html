The purpose of this study is to generate informed decision making capabilities on both the sides of the physician and osteoarthritis patient in order to better understand pain symptoms and proper treatment. This application will take passive and manually entered data from the osteoarthritis patient and provide the physician with a report on activity levels within a certain time series.

The product is a collaboration between Cornell Tech and Eli Lilly and Co. with technology support from Apple, Sage Bionetworks, and LifeMap Solutions. We expect that hundreds or thousands of people may join this study.

People with osteoarthritis do best when actively managing their daily life. This app uses your mobile phone to help you do this. If you take daily medication, the app will help you remember to take it on schedule. There are links to learn more about osteoarthritis, how to use your medicine, and how to recognize when you should see your doctor. The information you put into the app will help you track your activity levels and track your performance over time. Although we will look at the data at different points during the study, we will not be looking at your osteoarthritis information each day. The app does not replace your usual medical care, so if your condition worsens during the study, please reach out to your health care team.

By using this app, you will join a research study, so we will need you to formally give your consent. We will now tell you what is involved so you can make an informed choice. Take time to read the full written consent at the end of this signup process before making your choice. If you have any questions or anything is unclear, stop and email the study team at painoa@gmail.co before you sign the consent. If you contact us by email, you can also ask us to call you with more information or answers to your questions.

You cannot participate in this study if you:

  • Are younger than 18
  • Are pregnant

To join the study, you need to:

  • Have osteoarthritis confirmed by a doctor
  • Be prescribed medication for osteoarthritis
  • Live in the United States of America
