Symptoms can be associated with blood glucose levels being either too high (hyperglycemia) or too low (hypoglycemia).

Hyperglycemia (high blood glucose) may not result in immediate symptoms. When blood glucose levels are very high, people may experience a need to urinate frequently, feeling very thirsty, or blurry vision. Due to the long-term consequences of hyperglycemia, it is important to monitor your blood sugar according to the regimen suggested by your health care professional.

Hypoglycemia (low blood glucose) is usually defined as a glucose less than 70 mg/dL, but check with your care provider about the best threshold for you. Hypoglycemia can be life-threatening; some of the common symptoms associated with low blood glucose levels are:

  • Shakiness
  • Sweating, chills and clamminess
  • Confusion or delirium
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Lightheadedness or dizziness
  • Blurred or impaired vision
  • Tingling or numbness in the lips or tongue
  • Headaches
  • Weakness or fatigue
  • Lack of coordination
  • Seizures
  • Unconsciousness
